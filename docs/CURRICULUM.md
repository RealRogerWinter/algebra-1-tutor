# Curriculum

The finalized Algebra 1 course is a **hybrid**: the familiar traditional skills sequence, upgraded with the strongest moves from modern function‑forward curricula (Illustrative Mathematics, EngageNY/Eureka, CPM, Khan) and engineered around documented misconceptions.

The full machine-readable map (with the prerequisite graph and a placement check) lives in
[`algebra-1-tutor/references/curriculum-map.md`](../algebra-1-tutor/references/curriculum-map.md).

## What changed from a plain traditional sequence

- **Equals sign front‑loaded** as a relational *balance* in Unit 1 — the single most predictive early‑algebra misconception.
- **Functions introduced early (Unit 4)** and threaded through every later unit (lines, systems, quadratics are all narrated as functions), instead of stranded near the end.
- **Proportional reasoning reframed (Unit 3)** as the bridge to linearity: a unit rate *is* a constant rate of change *is* slope.
- **Modeling/translation** kept as a unit (Unit 6) and also woven throughout.
- **A light Sequences & Exponential Functions unit (Unit 9)** added — core modern Algebra 1 content the original sequence lacked.
- **Radicals folded into Quadratics (Unit 12)** rather than a standalone unit — they appear in service of the quadratic formula.
- **Statistics promoted to a core unit (Unit A)** — CCSS‑aligned data & statistics on the mastery path, kept trailing after the capstone so the spine numbering stays append‑only.
- **Prerequisite metadata** on every unit so a student can jump anywhere safely.

## Two dependency chains to keep solid

- distributive property → multiplying binomials (FOIL) → factoring → solving by factoring
- exponents → square roots → simplest quadratics → quadratic formula

## Misconceptions the course is engineered around

Documented, persistent, and predictive of algebra difficulty — each has a diagnostic and a repair in
[`misconceptions.md`](../algebra-1-tutor/references/misconceptions.md):

1. The equals sign: operational ("compute the answer") vs. relational ("the same as").
2. Variables: the letter‑as‑object error.
3. Negative numbers and the negative sign (subtracting a negative, `−5` vs `−2`, distributing a minus).
4. Fraction magnitude (bigger denominator ≠ bigger value; "reduce" doesn't shrink the value).
5. Order of operations: same‑tier, left‑to‑right.
6. Inequalities: forgetting the sign‑flip.
7. Algebraic structure / over‑applied "tricks" (e.g. forgetting the middle term in `(x+2)(x+3)`).
