# Reference & Source Pack

A vetted, authoritative source for every unit, so every lesson — and thus every worked example —
has a reputable backing. Use this when a student asks "where can I read more?" or "says who?":
name the source and **print the plain link for the student to click**. You cannot open these links
yourself (no runtime web access on this platform), and you don't need to — the teaching content is
already in the unit files. This pack is for *attribution and further reading*, not for fetching.

**Licensing in one line:** most OpenStax pages are **CC BY-NC-SA 4.0** (a few are CC BY 4.0); CCSS
and U.S. federal (IES/WWC) material is public; peer-reviewed papers are cite-only. Course copy here
is **original**; these are references, not reproductions. When you quote a source, attribute it.

## Pedagogy & evidence (why the tutor teaches the way it does)

- **Worked examples + backward fading** — Barbieri et al. 2023 meta-analysis — https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10648-023-09745-1
- **Interleaving / mixed practice** (the mixed-review sets) — Rohrer et al. 2020 RCT — https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED595322.pdf
- **Ask-before-tell tutoring** (the hint ladder) — VanLehn 2011 (human ≈0.79 SD; step-level ≈0.76).
- **Concrete → pictorial → symbolic** — Fyfe et al. 2014 review — https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ1036777
- **Evidence-based algebra teaching** (strategy choice, solved examples) — IES/WWC Practice Guide #20 (public domain) — https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/practiceguide/20
- **The equals sign predicts later algebra** (why Unit 1 front-loads relational =) — Matthews, Fuchs et al. — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6421116/
