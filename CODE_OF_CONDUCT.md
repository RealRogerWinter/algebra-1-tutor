# Code of Conduct

This project is a learning resource, and its community should feel safe and welcoming to everyone — learners, teachers, and contributors alike.

## Our standards

Everyone taking part is expected to:

- Be respectful, patient, and kind, especially toward beginners.
- Assume good faith, and give constructive, specific feedback.
- Welcome people of every background and level of experience.
- Keep the focus on what's best for learners and for the project.

These behaviors are not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Demeaning or discriminatory remarks.
- Publishing someone's private information without their permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussion or review.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other channel — and whenever someone is representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that falls short of these standards, please report it privately by contacting the maintainer (@RealRogerWinter) on GitHub. Reports will be reviewed and handled discreetly and in good faith.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions that don't meet these standards, and may warn — or, for repeated or serious problems, block — anyone who behaves unacceptably.
